Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

Version: 0.1
Jurisdiction: Ireland

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") sets out the terms governing the processing of Personal Data by a Processor on behalf of Dolphin Digital Group Ltd ("Controller") and, where applicable, its subsidiaries. This DPA should be read together with the parties' principal agreement(s).

1. Definitions

  • "Controller" means the entity which determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data (Dolphin Digital Group Ltd or a relevant subsidiary where explicitly stated).
  • "Processor" means the natural or legal person which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller under contract.
  • "Personal Data" has the meaning given in the GDPR and applicable data protection laws.
  • "Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed under the Agreement.

2. Purpose and Scope

This DPA governs processing of Personal Data carried out by the Processor on behalf of the Controller in connection with the parties' agreement(s). The Processor shall only process Personal Data as necessary to provide the contracted services and in accordance with the Controller's documented instructions.

3. Processor Obligations

  • The Processor shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller.
  • The Processor shall ensure that persons authorised to process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.
  • The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk (see Article 32 GDPR).
  • The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data breach affecting Personal Data processed under this DPA.
  • The Processor shall assist the Controller, as far as possible, in responding to requests from Data Subjects and in meeting Controller's obligations concerning data protection impact assessments and prior consultations with supervisory authorities.
  • The Processor shall maintain records of processing activities carried out on behalf of the Controller, and make such records available to the Controller and/or supervisory authorities on request where required by law.

4. Sub-processors

The Processor shall not engage additional sub-processors without the Controller's prior written authorisation. Where sub-processors are authorised or engaged, the Processor shall impose on them data protection obligations equivalent to those set out in this DPA and remain fully liable for their acts or omissions.

5. International Transfers

The Processor shall not transfer Personal Data outside the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom or Switzerland unless appropriate safeguards are in place (such as SCCs) and the Controller has been informed in advance.

6. Retention and Deletion

Upon termination or expiry of the services, and at the Controller's direction, the Processor shall delete or return all Personal Data and copies thereof, unless retention is required by applicable law. If the Processor is required to retain Personal Data by law, it shall notify the Controller and isolate the data from further processing.

7. Audits and Inspections

On reasonable notice, the Controller (or an auditor mandated by the Controller) may audit the Processor's compliance with this DPA. The Processor shall provide reasonable assistance and access to relevant information, logs and premises subject to confidentiality constraints and to the extent permitted by law.

8. Liability

Each party's liability arising under this DPA is subject to any limitations of liability and indemnities set out in the parties' principal agreement(s). Nothing in this DPA shall limit liability for wilful misconduct, fraud, or death/personal injury caused by negligence, or other liabilities that cannot be limited or excluded by applicable law.

9. Duration

This DPA remains in force for as long as the Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller under the parties' agreement(s).

10. Governing Law & Jurisdiction

This DPA is governed by the laws of Ireland. Disputes arising out of or in connection with this DPA shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Irish courts unless otherwise agreed in writing.
